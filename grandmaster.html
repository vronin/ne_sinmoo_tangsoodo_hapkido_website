Grandmaster John L. Godwin

10th Dan · Joong Kwang Sun Sa · Tang Soo Kwan

Background

Grandmaster John L. Godwin holds the rank of 10th Dan in Sin Moo Hapkido — the highest rank attainable beneath the founder. His title, Joong Kwang Sun Sa (Sun Sah Nim), designates him as a Supreme Grandmaster within the Sin Moo Hapkido hierarchy.

Early Training

Born circa 1961, Godwin grew up in Maple Shade, southern New Jersey. He began his martial arts training at Yi's Karate Institute in Pennsauken, NJ, studying Tang Soo Do, Moo Duk Kwan, and Hapkido under Master Ki Yun Yi. Training five to six days a week, he earned his first-degree black belt in 1982 and became a prominent competitor throughout the eastern United States, amassing over 150 first-place awards and numerous Grand Championship titles.

World Tang Soo Do Association

In 1983, Godwin joined Grandmaster Jae Chul Shin's World Tang Soo Do Association (WTSDA) headquarters dojang in South Philadelphia as full-time chief instructor and manager. GM Shin — who was Chuck Norris's first martial arts instructor — became a pivotal mentor.

Under Shin's guidance, Godwin opened Godwin's Korean Martial Arts Institute (KMAI) in Delaware in 1986 and eventually served as the WTSDA's Director of Business and Finance, helping grow the organization to over 400 schools in 40+ countries. He was named WTSDA Master of the Year in 2010.

Training Under Dojunim Ji Han-jae

The defining chapter of Godwin's career began in 1997 when he was introduced to Dojunim Ji Han-jae, the founder of Sin Moo Hapkido, through GM Ken MacKenzie. From that point forward, Godwin hosted weekly private instructor seminars with the Dojunim at his school in Delaware.

Over the following decades, Godwin accumulated over 300 private seminars with Ji Han-jae — more than any other practitioner in the world. Ji Han-jae publicly stated that Godwin was "one of the top Hapkido practitioners" and among those who "have enjoyed more mat-time with Dojunim than anyone in history."

In 2014, Ji Han-jae authorized Godwin to establish his own Tang Soo Kwan — a certified school style within Sin Moo Hapkido integrating his Tang Soo Do background with the full Sin Moo Hapkido curriculum. This places Godwin among approximately 19 Kwan holders worldwide.

International Achievements
  • 1999 Two Gold Medals and one Silver at the World Hapkido Games in Mok-Po, South Korea (U.S. Hapkido Team)
  • 2002 Gold Medal in full-contact fighting at the All-Korean Martial Arts World Championships, Olympic Park, Seoul
  • 2005 Participated in the first-ever Hapkido/Sin Moo Hapkido demonstration in Pyongyang, North Korea, alongside Dr. He-Young Kimm and Ken MacKenzie
  • 2010 Named WTSDA Master of the Year
  • 2011 Profiled in TaeKwonDo Times, "Chief Master John Godwin — 21st Century Warrior" (by Kenneth P. MacKenzie)
  • 2014 Authorized by Ji Han-jae to establish Tang Soo Kwan within Sin Moo Hapkido

GM Godwin has taught and demonstrated across North America, Europe, Africa, and Asia. He co-organizes the annual International Hapkido Summit in the Philadelphia / Southern New Jersey area.

Schools & Legacy

KMAI operates 8 locations across Delaware, Pennsylvania, and New Jersey, and has produced over 1,500 black belts and hundreds of instructors. All KMAI schools teach both Tang Soo Do and Sin Moo Hapkido as core curricula.

GM Godwin is listed on Wikipedia's Sin Moo Hapkido page as a 10th Dan Grandmaster and Tang Soo Kwan head. He remains one of the most active senior Hapkido instructors in the United States.

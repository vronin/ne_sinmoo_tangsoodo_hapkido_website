About the Art

Sin Moo Hapkido is a Korean martial art founded by the late Ji Han-jae, combining joint locking, throwing, striking, and kicking with breathing practice and meditation. It represents the complete expression of Hapkido as developed over decades of teaching and refinement.

Within this lineage, Tang Soo Kwan reflects John L. Godwin’s integration of Sin Moo Hapkido with his background in Tang Soo Do. The system maintains the core principles of Hapkido while preserving the structure and striking traditions of Tang Soo Do.

Training and Practice

North East SinMoo TangSoo Hapkido is composed of experienced martial artists, including black belts and senior practitioners with longstanding backgrounds in Tang Soo Do.

Training is conducted through ongoing practice and regular instruction under John L. Godwin. The group includes practitioners at multiple levels of advancement within Hapkido, with several holding black belt rank and others continuing their progression through the system.

The emphasis is on continued development, refinement of fundamentals, and deeper understanding of the principles underlying the art.
